# 🦬 Bison of the World

Welcome to the **Bison of the World** page. Bison are large grazing mammals found mainly in North America and Europe.

---

## American Bison

<div align="center">

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/8d/American_bison_k5680-1.jpg" width="500" alt="American bison">

</div>

### Scientific name

**Bison bison**

### Location

The American bison lives in parts of:

- The United States
- Canada
- Mexico

### Description

The American bison is the largest land mammal in North America. It has a large shoulder hump, a broad head, curved horns, and thick brown fur.

### Types of American Bison

#### Plains Bison

**Scientific name:** *Bison bison bison*

The plains bison traditionally lived throughout the grasslands and prairies of North America.

#### Wood Bison

**Scientific name:** *Bison bison athabascae*

The wood bison is generally larger and lives mainly in northern Canada and Alaska.

## European Bison

<div align="center">

<img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/26/Bison_bonasus_%28Linnaeus_1758%29.jpg" width="500" alt="European bison">

</div>

### Scientific name

**Bison bonasus**

### Other name

The European bison is also called the **wisent**.

### Location

European bison can be found in protected areas in several European countries, including:

- Poland
- Belarus
- Germany
- Romania
- Lithuania
- Ukraine
- Slovakia

### Description

The European bison is the largest wild land mammal in Europe. It usually has longer legs and a less noticeable shoulder hump than the American bison.

## Extinct Bison

Several other bison species lived in the past but are now extinct.

| Extinct species | Scientific name | General information |
|---|---|---|
| Steppe bison | *Bison priscus* | Lived across Europe, Asia, and North America |
| Ancient bison | *Bison antiquus* | Lived in prehistoric North America |
| Giant bison | *Bison latifrons* | A very large prehistoric bison with wide horns |
| Long-legged bison | *Bison occidentalis* | Lived in North America after the Ice Age |
| Southern bison | *Bison palaeosinensis* | An early bison species found in Asia |

## Why Bison Are Important

Bison help their ecosystems by:

- Grazing on grasses
- Creating habitats for smaller animals
- Spreading seeds
- Fertilizing the soil
- Supporting biodiversity
- Playing an important role in Indigenous cultures

---

## Fun Facts

- Bison can run much faster than they appear.
- Both male and female bison have horns.
- A group of bison is called a herd.
- American bison are often called buffalo, although they are not true buffalo.
- Bison calves are sometimes called red dogs because of their reddish-orange fur.
- Bison use dust baths called wallows to help remove insects and loose fur.
